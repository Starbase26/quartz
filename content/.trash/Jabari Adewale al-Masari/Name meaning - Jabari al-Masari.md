## Meaning of his name:

### Jabari
- **Language**: Swahili
- **Meaning**: Brave
- **Significance**: Suggests a fearless spirit—a seeker who dares to chart dangerous ley lines and explore hidden magical realms. It’s an excellent fit for a cartographer who embraces adventure.
### Adewale
- **Language** Yoruba (Nigeria)
- **Meaning**: “The crown has returned home” 
- **Significance**: Implies noble or royal heritage, perhaps suggesting that Jabari’s family was once displaced or nomadic but has now reclaimed its place in the magical world. It hints at legacy and purpose—he might see himself as someone who brings honor and knowledge back to his people.
### al-Masari
- **Language**: Arabic-inspired
- **Meaning**: “of the desert” or “the desert dweller”
- **Significance**: Rooted in Bedouin traditions, it emphasizes his nomadic ancestry—suggesting a connection to desert lands, wandering tribes, and ancient magical routes across shifting sands.
### Full Name meaning summary
Jabari Adewale al-Masari can be interpreted as:
- “The brave one in whom the crown has returned home, born of the desert.”
- Or more poetically:  
	- “A courageous seeker whose royal legacy finds its place among the sands.”
