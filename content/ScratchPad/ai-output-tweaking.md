Great question — and very sharp of you to notice. That "X is not Y, but Z" structure is a hallmark of academic or diplomatic writing, and while it clarifies nuance, it often **kills narrative flow** and weakens tone — especially in TTRPG writing where **directness, mood, and immersion** matter more than balance.

---

## How to Tweak My Output Style to Match Your Preference

To cut the contradictions and make writing punchier, instruct me to:

> **"Avoid contrast clauses like 'not this, but that.' Write with assertive, declarative tone. Prefer positive descriptions over negation or comparison."**

Here’s what that changes:

|**Old Style (default)**|**New Style (your preference)**|
|---|---|
|“The town isn’t peaceful, but rather tense and watchful.”|“The town is tense. Watchful eyes track every stranger.”|
|“He didn’t shout. Instead, he whispered with menace.”|“He whispered with menace, voice dry and deliberate.”|
|“The hall was not grand, but grim.”|“The hall stood grim, its stone silent and cold.”|

---

## Best Way to Lock in That Style (Custom GPT Instructions)

If you're building a Custom GPT, include this in the **"Instructions"** field:

> "Avoid negation-based contrasts ('not X, but Y'). Speak with a declarative, confident tone. Describe scenes and ideas in direct, evocative language. Do not hedge. Do not compare unless necessary. Use assertive imagery over nuance."

Or if you're not building a GPT yet, just drop a quick reminder in the chat like:

> "Write without using ‘not... but...’ constructions. Go direct."

---

## Bonus for TTRPG Use

This style works **especially well** for:

- Descriptions of environments, NPCs, artifacts
- Letters, in-world texts, faction propaganda
- Opening narration or boxed text
- Spells, curses, and ancient prophecies
