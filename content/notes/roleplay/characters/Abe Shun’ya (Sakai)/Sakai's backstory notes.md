# Back story notes

Having learned the mystical arts from his family, Sakai was able to peform the ritual of Taizan Fukun ceremony. A ceremony performed to the Lord of Death (Lord Taizan Fukun) to bring back a soul from the underworld.

Sakai’s future sire, was weary of life and wanted to life out one last lifetime in the sun, falling in love, abandoning the vampire world. When he read in ancient tomes of Old Japan, of the Omyoji, who had the ability to bring back the dead, he investigated, ultimately leading him to Sakai‘s family. 

For a while, he watched the Abe family. Having found out that this branch was a convergence of 2 lines leading back to Abe no Seimei (921-1005). {ELDER X} saved Shun’ya from death, after an attack on his family by the Yakuza. He did not want this bloodline to die, out of fear there would not be someone else capable of performing this mysterious right, of which he had only heard the name and purpose — ever anything more. 

Two decades later, keeping watch of Shun’ya’s life, he finally inserted himself into Shun’ya’s life. Leading him down the path to realise what had happened over 20 years ago, and who was responsible for Shun’ya’s survival. {ELDER X} was confident in Shun’ya’s character to know he would feel indebted to the elder which would require repayment. 

After a short wile of sharing time and lore with each other, {ELDER X} finally requested his payment: the Taizan Fukun-Sai. And since, Shun’ya was indebted, he ultimately, after some consideration, accepted and prepared the rite. 

During the ritual, the summoner must offer blood, a lot of blood, since to recall death, one must die. Shun’ya, not willing to sacrifice another, used his own blood for this. At the completion of the ritual, and just before {ELDER X} became mortal, he bestowed the gift onto a dying Shun’ya. 

An exchange of life between the two.
