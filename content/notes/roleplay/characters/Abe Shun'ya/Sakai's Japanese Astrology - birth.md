# 🌌 Onmyōdō Interpretation of Birth  
**Date:** Ansei 4, 2nd Month, 17th Day  
**Sexagenary Alignment:** 甲辰 (Kinoe-Tatsu)  
**Hour:** 辰の刻 (Tatsu no Koku, 7:00–9:00 AM)

---

## 1. Kanshi: 甲辰 (Kinoe-Tatsu)

- **Elemental Alignment:**
  - 甲 (Kinoe) = **Yang Wood (木の陽)** – Upright, growth-focused, expansive  
  - 辰 (Tatsu) = **Dragon** = Earth element, with hidden Water & Wood

- **Polarity:** **Yang–Yang** — active, generative, expansive

- **Symbolic Meaning:**  
  Kinoe-Tatsu marks a soul **sent down by Heaven with a mission** — one who must **shape, rule, or rebuild**. Dragons are celestial, boundary-crossing beings: part beast, part god.

- **In Onmyōdō terms:**
  - **Star Deity:** *Taizan Fukun (泰山府君)* – guardian of fates, tied to ancestral transitions
  - **Direction:** **East-Southeast (辰)** – auspicious for movement, growth, and spiritual elevation

---

## 2. Hour of the Dragon: 辰の刻 (7:00–9:00 AM)

- This doubles the presence of **辰 (Tatsu / Dragon)** in your chart.
- In Onmyōdō, such duplication **amplifies spiritual currents** and may rupture karmic limits.

- The Dragon hour is when **Heaven and Earth align**. Spirits move, and divine mandates (*tenmei* / 天命) are sealed.

> Born in **Tatsu no Koku on a Tatsu day**, one carries a **split fate** — between the **mortal** and the **divine**, between **ritual** and **rebellion**.

---

## 3. Gogyō (Five Phases / Elements)

- Dominant elements: **Wood (木)** and **Earth (土)**
- Wood nourishes Fire → Fire strengthens Earth. But here, **Wood is constrained by Earth**.
- Symbolically: **Life force trying to emerge from beneath weight** — a dragon **bearing the seed of the world tree**.

**Implications:**
- A destiny tied to **spiritual responsibility**
- A role as **rebuilder**, **ritualist**, or **legacy bearer**
- Potential for **great personal strength**, but also **inner pressure**

---

## 4. Divinatory Context: The Ansei Period

- Ansei 4 (1857) occurred during:
  - Foreign intrusion (post-Commodore Perry)
  - Ansei Purge (political suppression)
  - Earthquakes and celestial omens

- Onmyōji viewed this period as one of **kegare (穢れ)** — impurity and disrupted balance.

**Thus:**
- A child born in this time carries the karmic **burden of rebalancing**
- Likely to encounter (and confront) **disorder**, **decay**, or **injustice**
- A spiritual life marked by **restoration**, not escape

---

## 5. Ritual Recommendations (Based on Onmyōdō)

- **Auspicious Color:** *Aokiiro* (deep blue-green) — harmonizes Wood and latent Water
- **Favorable Direction:** *Southeast-East (辰)* — best for travel, shrine visits, or residence placement
- **Protective Kami:**
  - **Omoikane-no-Kami** – for clarity, intellect, and judgment
  - **Kifune Daimyōjin (貴船大明神)** – a water dragon deity of fate, storms, and spiritual authority

---

## 🔮 Summary (Onmyōdō Style)

> A soul born under **Kinoe-Tatsu in Tatsu no Koku**, during a time of cosmic upheaval, carries a **mandate not only to act**, but to **restore harmony** where others collapse in fear.  
This is the fate of the **ritual reformer**, the **dragon in human skin** — one born for **thresholds**, **storms**, and **subtle rule**.
