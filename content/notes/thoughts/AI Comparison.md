Absolutely, Kevin. While ChatGPT lacks a native "lorebook" or campaign tracker like NovelAI or World Anvil, you can **compensate — and even outperform — those systems** with the right setup. Here's how you can turn ChatGPT into a reliable campaign-tracking machine:

---

## 1. **Use Structured Notes via Uploads or Linked Documents**

Leverage the **file upload feature** in ChatGPT Plus (GPT-4-turbo):

- Upload your **world docs**, **NPC logs**, **session notes**, **timelines**, and **maps**.
- ChatGPT can read and **reference** this info directly in the chat (like a dynamic lorebook).
- Use formats like `.txt`, `.docx`, or `.md` for lore, and `.csv` for mechanical data (like NPC stats or loot tables).

🗂️ **Bonus Tip:** Keep a master "World Reference" file updated with core facts (pantheon, geography, factions, etc.), and just re-upload when changed.

---

## 2. **Create a Custom GPT with Memory and Instructions**

Use the **Custom GPT feature** to build your own persistent campaign assistant:

- Set system instructions like:
    > “You are a GM’s assistant for a dark fantasy campaign set in a war-torn archipelago. Always track major NPCs, factions, rumors, and ongoing quests.”
- Upload foundational lore files to your GPT.
- It will remember these until reset and treat them as gospel in all responses.

🔗 You can then talk to this GPT like a co-GM who already knows your world.

---

## 3. **Session Recap and Summarization**

After each session, paste or upload your session notes. Then ask:

> “Summarize the session in bullet points for continuity.”  
> “Update the faction status based on what happened.”  
> “Add rumors the players might now hear in taverns.”

This builds up a **living campaign memory** that you can call on whenever needed.

---

## 4. **Use Memory (When Available)**

ChatGPT's **memory feature** (rolling out widely) will eventually let it **retain knowledge** between chats — perfect for campaign continuity.

Example use:

> “Remember that King Raldor is secretly a vampire and was last seen in session 12 hiding in the sewers.”

Once enabled, this allows ChatGPT to organically bring up relevant lore and character facts — like a living DM journal.

---

## 5. **Supplement with External Tools**

If you want structured lore access but still want ChatGPT’s power:

- **Use Obsidian, Notion, or OneNote** to organize your lore (markdown or databases).
- ChatGPT can process exported files or even help generate them.
- Sync your written lore with your ChatGPT input via uploaded summaries or shared context.
